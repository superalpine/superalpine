French Alps

Discover the best of the French Alps with the Super Alpine guide to the ultimate places to visit and stay

Chamonix Mont-Blanc

Home to Europes highest peak and capital of mountain sports, Chamonix is a must stop on any alpine tour.

Le Cristal de Jade

The only luxury apartment complex in Chamonix, the Cristal de Jade is composed of three chalets, 51 apartments, a swimming pool and wellness center.

The well opionted apartments are all styled in a similar way with full kitchens for 2 people upwards. The wellness centre has a gym, hammam, sauna with a good sized indoor swimming pool and 2 jacuzzis. A spa is also available.

Situated in the heart of Chamonix next to the Aiguille du Midi lift station it has underground parking, bike store and laundry.

174 Allée des Aiguilles
74400 Chamonix-Mont-Blanc
France
Website

Aiguille du Midi

Featuring the spectacular 2 stage Téléphérique de l'Aiguille du Midi cable car ride, a trip to the Aiguille du Midi takes you has high as 3,777 metres with views of three countries and Mont Blanc.

100 Place de l'Aiguille du Midi
74400 Chamonix-Mont-Blanc
France
Website

Èze

The commune of Èze extends from the Mediterranean Sea at Èze-sur-Mer to the hilltop medieval village.

Hôtel Cap-Estel

Sitting on the a private lcoation on the spectacular Cap Estel, this 5 star hotel features elegant accommodation, beautiful gardens, a sea-water infinity pool and an indoor heated freshwater pool. Not to mention the private beach and fitness centre.

1312 Avenue Raymond Poincaré
06360 Èze
France
Website

Monaco

The prinicpality of Monaco packs a lot into less than one square mile of space. Home to more millionaires and billionaires than anywhere else, everything is available in this spectacular Mediterranean setting, squeezed into the edge of France.

Oceanographic Museum

Monaco’s Oceanographic Museum sits proudly on the west side of Le Rocher, the crown in the principality. It was founded by Prince Albert I in 1906 and is well known for its association with legendary undersea explorer Jacques Cousteau, who was its director from 1957 to 1988.

Avenue Saint-Martin
98000 Monaco
Website

Grenoble

The self-proclaimed Capital of the Alps Grenoble is a urban centre in the mountains, where art galleries and museums hug the banks of the Isère River.

Musée des Troupes de montagne

From the city centre a cable car takes you high above the town to the Fort de la Bastille with spectacular views of the city and surrounding mountains. The museum at the fort gives a history of the French Alpine troops.

Fort de la Bastille
38000 Grenoble
France

Antibes

Antibes is a resort town known for its old town enclosed by 16th-century ramparts with the star-shaped Fort Carré. This overlooks luxury yachts moored at the Port Vauban marina.

Hotel Du Cap-Eden-Roc
167-165 Boulevard J. F. Kennedy
06160 Antibes
France
