Swiss Passes

It was a mountain pass that brought together the communities that became Switzerland.

The Saint Gotthard pass was created in 1220 when the farmers of the region joined together to trade with the people of the Mediterranean. In August 1291 the first three cantons joined together to form the Helvetic Confederation - the official name of Switzerland. Thanks to the joining of these cantons, Switzerland is one of the most diverse countries in Europe. Today the 26 cantons that make up Switzerland are home to over 90 road passes, 50 of which are over 1200m in elevation - this is in addition to the many trail and rail passes.

Crossing the Alps used to be a life-threatening struggle against nature that has become increasingly easier thanks to conquering armies such as Napoleon's, wealthy landowners like Kasper Stockalper and the traders and farmers who helped build trails, tracks and later roads. All of today's passes have a hard surface with the Umbrail being the last to be fully surfaced in 2015. Many of the higher road passes have a rail or tunnel alternative and many feature road covers to limit disruption by the snow. The summits of today's passes are remnants of the past, with hostels used by ancient travellers becoming abandoned or converted into trinket shops or museums.
