Jaun Pass

Fribourg/Bern

The Jaun pass was completed in 1878 and is today one of the passes where you cross from German to French-speaking. It's another pass that was once important for national defence and during World War II the region was used for artillery fortifications that were disguised as alpine huts.

The pass starts just before the village of Boltigen. A steady climb from the turnoff takes you through grassy pastures, with cattle and goats enjoying the bright green fields. The Jaun pass is the greenest pass in Switzerland and it is not until after the summit on the descent you see anything other than grass and trees. A series of switchbacks with rock walls holding back the pastures increase the ascent to the top. The views are a classic Swiss scene with mountains in the distance. The summit plateau has a few buildings, restaurants and even a campsite. A short walk from the last parking area reveals a huge landscape. The descent is steeper with sheer cliffs to the village of Jaun.

Summit coordinates 46°35′27.0″N, 7°20′20.0″E
Summit Altitude 1509 metres
Distance 14 kilometres
Start Boltigen 843 metres
End Jaun 1047 metres
Opens All year*
* All year donates the pass is cleared of snow but maybe closed on some days
