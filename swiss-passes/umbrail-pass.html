Umbrail Pass

Graubünden/Italian border

The Umbrail Pass sits on the far side of Switzerland, the highest paved road in the country - it starts in Santa Maria Val Mustair and ends at the Italian border. The pass was completely tarmacked in 2015.

When Italy entered World War I in 1915, the Austro-Hungarian border became a major front and although they were not directly involved in the conflict, the Swiss had fortifications on the Umbrail Pass. The remains of these camps and fortifications are still viewable. A memorial stands on the pass.

Summit Location 46°32’35.0”N, 10°26’02.0”E
Summit Altitude 2503 metres
Distance 17 Kilometres
Start Santa Maria Val Mustair 1375 metres
End Italian border 2502 metres
Opens June to mid-October
