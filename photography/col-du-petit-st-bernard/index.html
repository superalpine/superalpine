Col du Petit St.Bernard

The frontier between France & Italy has long been fought over

The Col du Petit St.Bernard (Little St.Bernard pass) links the Aosta Valley in Italy with the Tarentaise Valley in France and has always been of critical value to both countries. It was back in the 11th century that Bernard of Menthon, an archdeacon in charge of caring for travellers, built the hostel that sits just below the passes 2188 metre high summit. Bernard first founded a hostel on what we now know as the Great St.Bernard Pass. Back in 1050, it was used by French and German pilgrims on their way to Rome and both passes were considerably more difficult to cross than today's road crossing.
